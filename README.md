## Project Overview

The Digital Literacy Portfolio is a project completed as part of my Digital Literacy course. The main aim of this project is to help students understand how to use digital tools effectively, safely, and responsibly in today’s world.

As a Student Digital Ambassador, I worked on different tasks that focus on real-life digital skills such as creating a professional online presence, using collaboration tools, writing formal emails, and understanding cyber safety. This project is not just an assignment, but also a learning experience that helped me improve my awareness of how the digital world works.

Through this portfolio, I explored various platforms and tools that are useful in both academic and professional life. It also helped me realize the importance of maintaining a responsible digital identity and using technology in a meaningful way.

---

## Project Modules

### Module 1: Digital Awareness Infographic
In this task, I created a one-page infographic using Canva to explain the concept of digital literacy. The infographic includes topics like safe internet practices, useful digital tools, professional online presence, and email etiquette. The aim was to present important information in a simple and visually clear format.

---

### Module 2: Professional Digital Profiles
In this module, I created and updated my profiles on platforms like GitHub, LinkedIn, and Kaggle. This helped me understand how to build a professional online presence. I also learned how these platforms are useful for sharing work, networking, and improving skills.

---

### Module 3: Coding & Collaboration Platforms
For this task, I solved a beginner-level coding problem on HackerRank, which helped me improve my basic programming skills. I also created a "Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz" using Google Forms and linked it with Google Sheets to collect responses. This task showed me how digital tools can be used for collaboration and data collection.

---

### Module 4: Email Etiquette & Communication
In this module, I learned how to write professional emails. I created two emails—one for requesting an assignment extension and another for applying for an internship. I also prepared a checklist of social media do’s and don’ts, which helped me understand how to communicate responsibly online.

---

### Module 5: Cybercrime Awareness
In this task, I studied a cybercrime case related to phishing and identity theft. I also created a prevention checklist to help students stay safe online. This task made me more aware of online threats and how to avoid them.
